About Azadamard Scouting

Founded in 1977, Homenetmen Pasadena “Azadamard” Scouting has proudly carried forward a tradition of scouting, leadership, and Armenian heritage for generations of youth in our community. For nearly five decades, our scouts have gathered to learn the values of teamwork, discipline, service, and respect for nature, while building lifelong friendships and unforgettable memories. Through camping trips, outdoor skills, cultural education, and community service, Azadamard continues to shape confident, responsible young leaders who are proud of their roots and prepared to serve their communities. Guided by dedicated volunteers and the spirit of Homenetmen, our scouting family remains committed to developing strong character, adventurous minds, and a lasting love for scouting.

New scouts are always welcome.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers for new families interested in Azadamard Scouts.

What ages can join?

Ages 4 and up. Scouts are placed in the appropriate group based on age.

Can my child try before registering?

Yes. We highly recommend coming for a trial Sunday before registering so your child can experience scouting and meet the leaders.

What do they wear?

A plain T-shirt or anything with an Armenian affiliation is perfectly fine. A full uniform is not required to start.

What should my child bring?

A water bottle, comfortable clothes, and closed-toe shoes. We recommend clothes appropriate for outdoor activities.

Are parents required to stay?

Parents are welcome to stay and observe, but it is not required. Many parents drop off and return at pickup.
